Random Forest Classification

We will choose the two most frequent families of trees and use the random forest classifier machine learning technique. The classifier will take the diameter at breast height and the year that is was planted and the location it is planted and return the family of the tree.

Diameter at breast height (otherwise known as DBH), is the standard for measuring trees. It refers to the tree diameter measured at 4.5 feet (approximately 1,4 meters) above the ground.

As mentioned earlier, column Diameter Breast Height has high rates of missing values. Because of the volume of the dataset, when dropping the missing values, we still had a large amount of data to train and test our model. (23535 rows).

Evaluate the model

70% of the data was used for training and 30% test. We selected to evaluate the model we built via the accuracy, the cross validation score and confusion matrix.

The accuracy of the model is 71.6 %

0.72 accuracy with a standard deviation of 0.01

The confusion matrix allowed us to visualize the performance of the classifier. It can be seen that the model made 3576 predictions. From those, the model predicted correctly 2259.

Is the model overfitting?

Overfitting occurs when the model learns the training set very well but is unable to generalize its knowledge to the test set.

The difference between the accuracies is marginal, so the classifier is not overfitting.
