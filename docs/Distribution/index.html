Distribution of the Trees

Published May 9, 2021 by in Demo at https://veronica-desantos.github.io/Distribution/

Fundamental distributions of the data.

To have an insight of the data and vizualize the basic distributions of them, we asked ourselves a sequence of questions we wanted to answer.

Yearly patterns

Most trees were planted during the year 1998, with years 2000 and 1997 following.

Monthly patterns

It can be seen that most trees are planted from October to January. This make perfect sense since, in the Southern hemisphere, those months are the meteorolgical period of spring and summer.

Monthly patterns for different families

We wanted to know if Is there any correlation between the months and different families of trees.

The dataset includes 61 different families of trees. We got the 14 most frequent families and tried to detect which month is prefered to plant trees that belong in a certain family.

Our findings are presented in the list below.

Families that were planted during fall and winter in Melbourne:
Family Month
Myrtaceae, Fabaceae March
Casuarinaceae,Malvaceae, Rosaceae, Proteaceae July
Anacardiaceae June
Families that were planted during spring and summer in Melbourne:
Family Month
Sapindaceae December
Ulmaceae,Moraceae,Fagaceae November
Platanaceae October
